Screening Methodology

üí∞ Valuation Metrics

We analyze key valuation ratios to identify undervalued stocks:

  • P/E Ratio: Price relative to earnings. Lower values may indicate undervaluation.
  • P/B Ratio: Price relative to book value. Useful for assessing asset value.
  • PEG Ratio: P/E relative to growth rate for growth perspective.

üìä Financial Health

We evaluate the financial strength and profitability of companies:

  • ROE (Return on Equity): Measures how effectively the company generates profits from shareholder equity.
  • Debt/Equity Ratio: Indicates financial leverage and risk level.
  • Current Ratio: Assesses short-term liquidity and ability to pay obligations.

üéØ Income & Growth

We look for sustainable income and growth characteristics:

  • Dividend Yield: Annual dividend payout as percentage of stock price.
  • Earnings Growth: Year-over-year and multi-year revenue/earnings trends.
  • Free Cash Flow: Cash available after capital expenditures for distribution.

üè¢ Market & Sector Analysis

Contextual factors for comprehensive evaluation:

  • Market Cap Segmentation: Categories from micro to mega cap stocks.
  • Sector Performance: Relative strength within industry peers.
  • 52-Week Performance: Recent price movement indicating momentum or recovery.

‚ö†Ô∏è Risk Assessment

Understanding potential risks in investment selection:

  • Volatility: Stock price fluctuation as measure of risk.
  • Beta: Sensitivity to market movements (Beta > 1 = more volatile).
  • Debt Levels: High leverage increases financial risk during downturns.

üîÑ Screening Workflow

Our systematic approach to stock selection:

  1. Select sector and market cap parameters
  2. Filter by valuation metrics (P/E, P/B ratios)
  3. Apply financial health criteria
  4. Review dividend and growth metrics
  5. Assess risk parameters
  6. Shortlist candidates for further analysis
